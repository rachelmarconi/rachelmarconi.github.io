Hello! I'm Rachel, an interactives designer and developer.

I chew on complex problems. I make data human-legible and local-relatable. I love to learn new things and stretch the limits of what's doable.

Most recently, I built interactive visuals for the Urban Institute, a nonprofit in the policy research space. I worked on large data explorers and dashboards on teams with writers and designers, mid-sized rapid response features on a tight budget and timetable, and small, one-off charts and tables in Datawrapper for a quick-learning blog team.

I got my start as a city council reporter with a computer engineering degree in rural Pennsylvania, then got a master's in journalism with a focus in data and web development, simultaneously covering health, business, and local life in Maryland. I made interactive experiences at USATODAY out of grad school before two years working with Svelte 4 and 5 at Urban.

I'm eager to dive into important work building ways for folk to understand the issues around them. I'm invested in humans. And the work is worth it.

It's great to meet you!
